Fried Rice

Description

A dish originating from Central Asia during the Han dynasty, fried rice is a staple dish in many households around the world.

While today we primarily use chicken and vegetables, fried rice originally contained muxu (also known as alfalfa), giving it the name "muxufan"

What happened after that is up for debate, but most people DO agree that if you have leftover vegetables and rice -- fried rice is the go-to dish.

containing mostly vegetables and a pinch of protein, fried rice is also IBS friendly -- and delicious too!

Ingedients

  • 1-2 tablespoons butter (you can use vegetable oil if butter triggers your IBS!)
  • 1-2 tablespoons garlic infused olive oil (to taste)
  • 1 inch knob of ginger, finely diced
  • 1 chilli, finely sliced
  • 4 tablespoons soy sauce (or more to taste)
  • 2 tsp oyster sauce
  • 1/2 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 large eggs, whisked
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and diced
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • 1/2 bunch of green onions, green section only
  • 2 chicken breasts, cubed (or tofu, if vegetarian)
  • 4 cups cooked rice -- preferrably at least a day old
  • Any other vegetables you have lying around the fridge!

Steps

  1. Heat 1/2 of either butter or garlic oil in large wok over medium heat.
  2. Add egg -- cook until scrambled. Break up into very small places. Remove to seperate plate.
  3. Add remaining butter or garlic oil to wok. Add ginger and chili -- cook until fragrant.
  4. Saute for ~ 6 minutes (or until veggies have some give)
  5. Add rice and green onions on top of veggies. Pour some of soy and oyster sauce over rice to help break up clumps. Pour remainder around the edge.
  6. Fry the rice for 4-5 minutes -- make sure to let the rice rest a bit to get nice and crispy, but don't burn it!
  7. When rice is cooked to your liking, add eggs and mix.
  8. Remove from heat and add sesame oil -- mix gently.
  9. Serve immediately!
