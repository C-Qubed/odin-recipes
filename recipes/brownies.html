Brownies

Description

A dish originating from Central Asia during the Han dynasty, fried rice is a staple dish in many households around the world.

While today we primarily use chicken and vegetables, fried rice originally contained muxu (also known as alfalfa), giving it the name "muxufan"

What happened after that is up for debate, but most people DO agree that if you have leftover vegetables and rice -- fried rice is the go-to dish.

containing mostly vegetables and a pinch of protein, fried rice is also IBS friendly -- and delicious too!

Ingedients

  • 2/3 cup gluten free flour
  • 1 1/2 tsp backing powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 sticks unsalted butter -- use microwave to partially melt
  • 10 oz dark chocolate -- the higher cocoa % the better
  • 4 oz unsweetened chocolate chips
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup nuts -- pecans or walnuts work best/li>

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375F. Line baking pan with parchment paper (We like 13 x 9!).
  2. Whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt in large mixing bowl.
  3. Combine butter and dark chocolate in a medium bowl. Microwave until melted.
  4. In another large bowl, whisk together eggs, sugar, and vanilla. Add chocolate & butter mix.
  5. Fold in the dry mixture until flour is well-integreted. Fold in chocolate chips after.
  6. Pour batter into pan -- sprinkling nuts on top.
  7. Bake for 25 minutes, then check if done using a toothpick (or butterknife, if you're like us).
  8. When toothpick comes out clean, brownies are done. Cool for 5-10 minutes, cut, and enjoy!
