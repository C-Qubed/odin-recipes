Pad Thai

Description

Created in the 1930's in Thailand by Plaek Philbunsongkhram, Pad Thai is a noodle dish loved around world.

There are many variations on the original recipe, but in its most basic form, Pad Thai is rice noodles, various vegetables, and a source of protein.

The recipe listed here is a twist on the original recipe that is low FODMAP to accomodate people with IBS.

Ingedients

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp maple syrup
  • 2 Tbsp peanut butter
  • 1 tsp fish sauce (or more soy, if vegetarian)
  • 250g rice noodles
  • 2 tbsp garlic infused olive oil
  • 2 chicken breasts, cubed (or tofu, if vegetarian)
  • 2 eggs, whisked
  • 2 tsp ginger, finely chopped
  • 1 chilli, finely slided
  • 2 zucchini, made into veggie noodles
  • 1 carrot, made into veggie noodles
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/2 bunch of green onions, green section only
  • 1 cup cilantro leaves
  • peanuts, extra bean sprouts, and lime slices (to serve)

Steps

  1. In a small bowl, whisk soy sauce, maple syrup, peanut butter, lime juice, and fish sauce.
  2. Boil water and add rice noodles -- 4 minutes for al dente, longer to taste.
  3. In a wok (or a large pan), heat 1 tbsp of garlic infused oil on medium-high heat and add chicken. Cook chicken until seared, turning occasionally.
  4. Turn heat to medium. Add whisked egg, tossing the chicken around so that is coated in the egg. Cook for 5-6 minutes (or until chicken is done)
  5. Remove chicken and egg from pan and put aside. Heat remaining oil along with ginger and chili until fragrant.
  6. Add zucchini & carrot noodles. Add bean sprouts. Toss together.
  7. Re-add chicken and egg to pan. Add rice noodles on top. Toss vigousouly with wok -- two forks are helpful if noodles are clumped to create space.
  8. Pour the sauce mixture over the mix. Stir through. Add green onion and cilantro on top.
  9. Serve with extra bean sprouts, crushed peanuts, and lime slices on the side.
